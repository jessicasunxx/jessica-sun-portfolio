Soldering Sculpture

A small soldered sculpture using a 12-pin header and bent brass rod to explore form, stability, and connection in three-dimensional space.

Materials

  • 12-pin male header
  • Brass rod, cut into short segments
  • Soldering iron and solder
  • Helping hands / third-hand tool
  • Small perfboard base (optional for extra stability)
  • Flush cutters and pliers

Process

  • Sketched a few silhouettes focusing on balance and negative space.
  • Cut and bent brass rods into arcs and straight segments that could span between header pins.
  • Tacked one connection at a time, using helping hands to hold the structure while solder cooled.
  • Layered additional rods to create crossing lines and subtle asymmetry without losing stability.
  • Cleaned excess flux and refined joints so the solder itself became part of the visual language.

Photos

Reflection

This project turned soldering from a purely functional skill into a way of drawing in three dimensions. Working at this small scale made every bend and joint feel consequential, because a millimeter shift could change the center of gravity or the silhouette completely.

I learned how much control is needed to place heat and solder precisely without melting previous connections, and how tools like helping hands become part of the design process rather than just supports. If I iterate on this idea, I would like to push the structure taller and more precarious while still keeping it self-supporting, and experiment with introducing light or motion into the form.
